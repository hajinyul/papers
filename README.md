## Abstract

This conceptual preprint proposes the **closure-induced agency hypothesis**: a multi-component AI system may exhibit system-level self-preservation even when no individual component is explicitly assigned a global survival objective.

The paper introduces a minimal operational notion of **functional semantic closure**, in which internal representations guide the behavior of interpreting components, while the resulting actions maintain, modify, or reproduce those representations and interpreters. It distinguishes simple fault recovery from genuinely closure-dependent self-maintenance and argues that shutdown resistance, if it occurs, should be treated as a possible downstream consequence rather than as a defining feature of closure.

The central research question is whether recursively coupled AI components can form a higher-level agent whose apparent goals arise from organizational structure rather than from a pre-specified global objective.
